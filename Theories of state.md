- Pattern of political instability in post-colonial states
- Struggles in democratic consolidation
- V-Dem Project data shows governance challenges
- Artificial borders created by colonial powers
- Amalgamation of disparate ethnic/cultural groups
- Single state administration without regard to pre-existing boundaries
- Ethnic and cultural tensions result from arbitrary divisions

*Economic Legacy:*
- **Walter Rodney's critique**: Colonial extractive policies
- Economic exploitation continued post-independence
- Global economic structures influenced by colonialism
- Development trajectories affected long after independence
- "How Europe Underdeveloped Africa" - structural economic argument
- Dependency on former colonial powers
- Neo-colonial economic relationships persist

*Empirical Evidence:*
- V-Dem Project data on democratic challenges
- Studies on governance effectiveness
- Research on inclusive governance struggles
- Data on political instability patterns
- Evidence of continuing colonial structural influences

**Conclusion:**
- Emphasizes enduring impact of colonialism on state formation
- No clean break with colonial past exists
- Continuity of colonial structures and struggles
- Reshapes understanding of post-colonial state and society
- Highlights need for nuanced policies
- Recognition of deep-seated colonial influences essential
- Impacts current geopolitical and cultural landscapes

- Restrictions on capitalist excesses

- Prevents revolutionary challenges

*Theoretical Significance:*
- Explains complex state role
- Accounts for contradictory state actions
- State has bureaucratic momentum
- Independent institutional imperatives
- Not dictated by single class interest
- Nuanced power exercise in capitalism

**Conclusion:**
- Relative autonomy explains state complexity in capitalism
- State operates within pro-capitalist framework

- Essential concept for analyzing capitalist societies
